Ontario has more invasive species than any other province or territory in Canada. There are more than 180 invasive aquatic animals in the Great Lakes and more than 440 invasive plants in Southern Ontario. These non-native flora and fauna present mild-to-severe risks to local ecosystems and to the Ontarian economy. Our mission is to help you identify any invasive species you may encounter from your camping trips to Muskoka to your walks in Rouge Park.
